I. Administrative Review (HSA)



Initial review of pharmacy and any non-compliance or partial compliance items will be addressed on the attached follow-up Action Plan (CAP) Form:
1 Continous Quality Improvement (CQI) / Risk Management conducted and reviewed quarterly by CQI committee
2 All records and regulatory visits are current, organized and accesible for audits
3 Weekly DON Pharmacy Spot Check List is conducted
4 Pharmacy & Therapeutics Committee minutes are up-to-date and reviewed with appropriate staff and committees.
5 Staff training records regarding medication administration, security and accountability are available and up to date
6 HSA understands vendor flash reports, invoices and FMR reports
7 HSA understands pharmacy budget reports
II. Administrative Review (Pharmacy Room)
Review pharmacy procedures and physical items
8 Check for site-specific Pharmacy Policy & Procedure Manual and accessibility to staff.
a) Verify that all applicable staff is aware of the contents of their Pharmacy Vendor Binder and trained on important aspects. (Backup Pharmacy Process, Reverse Distributor, Release Meds, etc)
9 Licenses



a) Is the Pharmacy License posted and up to date?
b) Is the DEA license posted and up to date?
c) Is the Consultant Pharmacist License posted and up to date?
d) Is the Pharmacy Vendor Licenses posted and up to date?
10 Pharmacy Binders - review each binder use with pharmacy nurse



a) Manifest Binder (Pharmacy Manifest is compared with medications received and checked off?)
b) Returns Binder - Unused or outdated medications are returned to pharmacy or destroyed according to state regulations
c) Controlled Substance Destruction Binder
d) Consultant Pharmacist Binder readily accesible and up to date?
III. Pharmacy Overview



Review pharmacy procedures and physical items
11 Physical Items



a) Narc box for storage of Controlled Substances should be double locked
Review the Controlled Substances Record. Is documentation being done appropiately? (Shift Counts, Adding to Inventory, Administrations, etc)

Spot check controlled substances - Is the count correct?
Discontinued Controlled Substances remain on count until properly disposed.
b) Refrigerator temperature is properly documented using the appropiate form (between 36-46 degrees)
Multi doses vials are dated and initialed (Discarded within the appropiate time period)
Medication Refrigerator contains ONLY medication
Refrigerated Controlled Substances are properly stored (ie. Double locked)
c) Does the site have a Pharmaceutical Waste Container? Is the site aware of how to process a return for waste?
12 Medication Room



a) There is a perpetual inventory method in place to account for stock meds
b) Internal/External meds are stored separately, opthalmic and otologic meds are separated
c) Overall apperance of the pharmacy is clean, organized and clutter free
d) Personal bags are not stored in the pharmacy room or medcarts
e) No food or drinks inside the pharmacy room or stored inside the med or treatment carts
f) Current Drug reference books including drug handbook, pharmacy manual, formulary, PDR or Facts and Comparisons, Pharmacy Law and rules in hard copy or in readily accesible electronic formart available
g) Does the site have a PEPKIT?
h) Emergency bag/box is labeled, reviewed and documented monthly - No expired meds
13 Medcart & Medpass



a) Are there any outdated/expired medication(s) found on medication cart
b) Medication is documented using the MAR/eMAR/EMR directly following administration
c) Medications are prepared for prepouring only for second tier, segregation, and other approved areas, where medication carts are unaccessible.
d) Medication administration records are completed, no blank spaces, signed by nurse administering medications
e) Refusals are properly documented on MAR and Refusal Form signed and followed up accordingly
f) Patient personal medication(s) are kept with personal property or approved location
g) Are medication carts locked /secured
h) Floorstock and/or patient specific medications are not defaced/misbranded
